# Leviathan Alpha — 30-Day Marketing Plan

\textcolor{signalgreen}{\textbf{Mission:}} Build a credible voice in prediction-market intel during the 30 days before launch. Grow waitlist + Twitter following + Sunday newsletter (The Leviathan) audience without a live product.

**Constraints**
- No live product. All content is commentary, screenshots/mockups, market analysis.
- Brand account only (`@leviathanalpha` or similar). No founder account.
- Twitter/X primary, Threads cross-post.
- $500–$1k budget for boosting top-performing posts in weeks 3–4.
- One newsletter issue every Sunday (4 issues over 30 days).

---

## Strategy Overview

### The wedge
You're not "another analytics tool." You're a **point of view**: the only account that synthesizes Polymarket, Kalshi, Manifold whale flow, cross-venue arbs, and catalyst calendars in one place. The brand voice is **terse, technical, contrarian** — Bloomberg terminal energy, not crypto-influencer energy.

### Three content pillars (every tweet fits one)

1. **Tape (40%)** — Real, current prediction-market data. Whale moves, cross-venue spreads, unusual volume, market opens, resolution risk.
2. **Take (40%)** — Opinionated reads on the week's events. "Here's what the smart money is pricing that mainstream news is missing."
3. **Behind-the-curtain (20%)** — Building Leviathan Alpha in public. Feature teases, mockup screenshots, dev updates, philosophy posts.

### Cadence
- **1–3 tweets per day.** Quality > quantity. If a day has no signal, post one observation. Don't force three.
- **Sunday 8 AM ET: newsletter drops.** Promote it Mon (recap), Wed (one nugget), Fri (next-Sunday tease).
- **Threads:** auto-cross-post via Meta's tool, but no native engagement. Cross-post only the tweets that don't lean heavily on threaded replies.

### What we are NOT doing
- No memes (off-brand)
- No reply-guying to mega-accounts unsolicited
- No engagement farming ("RT if you agree")
- No celebrity name-drops without substance
- No financial advice. Ever.

## Risks & Mitigations

**Risk: Posting too much, becoming noise.**
Cap at 3 tweets per day, hard. Better to under-post.

**Risk: Looking like a crypto-shill account.**
Avoid emoji clusters, avoid "WAGMI"/"GM" culture. Stay terse, technical, Bloomberg-energy.

**Risk: Posting wrong data publicly.**
Always double-check Polymarket/Kalshi data before tweeting. If you're wrong, correct it transparently within the hour.

**Risk: Getting into a fintwit fight.**
Don't quote-dunk on people. The brand voice is observational, not confrontational. Make corrections, not attacks.

**Risk: Newsletter open rate drops.**
Subject line is everything. Test 2–3 subject lines per issue if your email tool supports it.
